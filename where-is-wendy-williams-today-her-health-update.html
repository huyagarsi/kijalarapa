Where Is Wendy Williams Today &amp; Her Health Update? Her Illness Explored

Where Is Wendy Williams Today & Her Health Update? For the entirety of season 14, Wendy Williams has been gone from The Wendy Williams Show. Since the season debuted, several celebrities have stepped in as guest hosts as Williams battled health difficulties.

Wendy Williams is an American journalist, broadcaster, and media personality. From 2008 to 2022, she presented the nationally syndicated talk show The Wendy Williams Show.

Williams began her career as a radio DJ and broadcaster, immediately earning the nickname “shock jockette” in New York. She rose to notoriety as the topic of The Wendy Williams Experience, a 2006 VH1 reality show that followed events around her radio show.

Where Is Wendy Williams Now? 

Wendy Williams announced in November that she was eager to return to her talk show after taking a break due to a variety of health issues.

Williams had a difficult few months following a Covid-19 diagnosis and ongoing health issues.

A source exclusively told The Sun that finding replacements for Wendy, who recently purchased a NYC penthouse despite her health issues, had been difficult.

Sherri Shepherd was supposed to guest host The Wendy Williams Show on December 13, 2021, but she had to be replaced by Michael Rapaport due to appendicitis.

Remy Ma and Fat Joe took over for Devyn Simone, Elizabeth Wagmeister, Bevy Smith, Whitney Cummings, and Michael Yo before Shepherd and Rapaport.

According to The Wrap, as the show continued its season without Williams, it saw a 33 percent increase in household ratings, ranking fifth among syndicated talk shows, just behind Dr. Phil, Live With Kelly and Ryan, The Ellen DeGeneres Show, and The Kelly Clarkson Show.

Wendy Williams Health Update- Her Illness Explored 

According to Wendy Williams’ representative, the star has been dealing with “challenging health issues” that have caused her to step away from The Wendy Williams Show. Williams previously disclosed her Graves’ disease diagnosis, and she has taken extended breaks from her show in 2018, 2019, 2020, and 2021.

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, the actress also has lymphedema, which is swelling caused by a buildup of lymph fluid in the body (CDC).

While season 13 of The Wendy Williams Show was scheduled to premiere in the fall of 2021, Williams decided in September to cancel all public appearances promoting her show. One month later, she revealed that she had been diagnosed with COVID-19 and that the new season would be postponed due to “ongoing medical issues.”

Williams provided an update on her health in a video posted to her new personal Instagram account. While on a beach walk with her son Kevin Hunter Jr., Williams stated that she was “doing fine” as she spends time with family in Florida.

What Happened To Wendy Williams? 

Throughout February, The Wendy Williams Show has been led by guest hosts as Williams deals with ongoing health issues. Since October 2021, the show has aired without Williams, with fan favorites such as Michael Rapaport, Terrence J, Fat Joe, Remy Ma, Bevy Smith, and Sherri Shepherd filling in.

Following much speculation about the show’s future, a new Instagram post on the formal Wendy Williams Show account on Tuesday declared that Shepherd will take over Williams’ FOX time slot with her own daytime show.

Shepherd also announced the news on her personal Instagram account, saying she’s always wanted to have her own TV show
