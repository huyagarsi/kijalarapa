Kim Kardashian Responds To Taylor Swift's Diss On TTPD, Says She Should &quot;Be Over It&quot; By Now

Highlights

  • Kim Kardashian wants Taylor Swift to move on from their feud, but fans are siding with Taylor.
  • Taylor's new song subtly references the pain caused by Kim and Kanye's actions during the feud.
  • Taylor Swift's emotional response to the public feud shows how deeply it affected her mental health.

While it's been eight years since the famous Taylor Swift, Kanye West, and Kim Kardashian feud, the aftermath of it seems to still affect Taylor. There were a lot of references to people in her life on Taylor's new album, some for better, some for worse, and she took a clear dig at Kim Kardashian in the song "thanK you aIMe."

Taylor Swift recently dove into the meaning behind her new songs and mentioned a few of them being about toxic relationships.

After keeping silent for a couple of days about Taylor Swift's diss, Kim Kardashian finally seems to have reacted. She is apparently asking the pop star to stop referring to her in songs, as she thinks she should be "over it" by now because it's been years, but this request backfired spectacularly, as people online have been referencing the scandal more than ever to remind Kim of how much Taylor was hurt during that time.

Kim Kardashian Wants Taylor Swift To 'Move On'

According to a source, the reality TV star was bothered by the song "thanK you aIMee", but not exactly because of the lyrics attacking her. Reportedly, she just wants to move on from their feud, and is tired of Taylor Swift referencing it.

"She’s over it and thinks Taylor should move on," the source said. Apparently, Kim Kardashian "doesn’t get why [Swift] keeps harping on it" since "it's been literally years."

The song "thanK you aIMee" doesn't mention the SKIMS founder by name, but it wasn't difficult for fans to identify the name the seemingly random capital letters in the title formed.

Kim Kardashian knows how to leverage her fame, so it's no wonder her divorce has barely made a dent in her net worth.

"All that time you were throwing punches / I was building something / And I can't forgive the way you made me feel / Screamed 'F**k you, Aimee' to the night sky / As the blood was gushing / But I can't forget the way you made me heal," the song lyrics read.

Taylor Swift also seemed to mention the way their public feud affected her family, and how it's still affecting her to this day.

"Everyone knows that my mother is a saintly woman / But she used to say she wished that you were dead / I pushed each boulder up the hill / Your words are still just ringing in my head, ringing in my head."

Fans Defended Taylor Swift Not Moving On From The Fight

Kim Kardashian's response to the song wasn't well received online, and not only by swifties. Even people who admittedly aren't Taylor Swift fans are saying that, for Taylor to move on, Kim Kardashian should apologize for the harm she caused the singer.

For those who don't remember the famous feud, when Kim Kardashian was married to Kanye West, she released an edited phone call to "prove" that Taylor Swift had authorized the rapper to name drop her in a song where he said he "made that b*tch famous." After the fake phone call was leaked, Taylor was relentlessly bullied, to the point where she had to leave the country and fell into a deep depression.

They are referring, of course, to the music video Kanye West made that featured a naked doll of Taylor Swift. His and Kim Kardashian's actions, added to the aggression she received from their followers, changed Taylor's life forever.

"That took me down psychologically to a place I’ve never been before. I moved to a foreign country. I didn’t leave a rental house for a year. I was afraid to get on phone calls. I pushed away most people in my life because I didn’t trust anyone anymore. I went down really, really hard."

Knowing all this, it's unlikely Taylor will move on from the incident any time soon.
