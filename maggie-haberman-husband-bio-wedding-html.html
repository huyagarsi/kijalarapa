Maggie Haberman Bio: Age & Family

Born on 30 October 1973 to a Jewish family Maggie Lindsey Haberman graced into the world as the daughter of Clyde Haberman and Nancy Haberman. Her father was The New York Times journalist while her mother was a media communications executive at Rubenstein Associates.

She attended Ethical Culture Fieldston School and later graduated from Sarah Lawrence College with a bachelor’s degree.

How Much Salary Does Maggie Haberman Earn From CNN And The NY Times?

Coming to her professional life, she started her journalism career in 1996 after being hired by The New York Post. Soon in the early 2000s, she started working for the New York Daily News.

Later, she returned to The NY Post in 2008 to cover the U.S presidential campaign and other various political issues. Then, in 2010 Politico hired her as a senior reporter but she left the publication to work for CNN as a political analyst in 2014.

She returned to The New York Times in 2015 as a political correspondent for 2016 U.S Presidential Campaign and since then has been serving NY Times currently as a White House Correspondent.

Maggie’s along with her colleagues at NY Times and Washington Post won the 2018 Pulitzer Prize for National Reporting reporting on the Trump administration.
