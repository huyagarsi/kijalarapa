He shouldnt have won, Joe Rogan and Dave Portnoy have heated moment debating Paddy Pimblett

On his most recent podcast, Joe Rogan confronts David Portnoy about the decision going Paddy Pimblett’s way, saying he didn’t deserve to win it.

Joe Rogan when asked by David Portnoy, the Barstools Sports president, about the Paddy Pimblett fight, said that he didn’t think Paddy Pimblett deserved to win. “Ehhh he shouldn’t have won…I thought Jared Gordon won that fight”, said Joe Rogan. David Portnoy then said that he thought the fight was close, but Joe Rogan didn’t falter and maintained that Jared Gordon won that fight.

UFC 282 has become famous for its controversial decision in the Paddy Pimblett vs. Jared Gordon fight which went the Scouser’s way in a split decision win. The decision wasn’t very well received by many fighters and they took to Twitter to say that the decision was “disgraceful”. Paddy Pimblett has been losing fans for not accepting or even acknowledging the split decision, he maintained that the fight was not even close and he was the “clear” winner.

When David Portnoy asked Joe Rogan about the fight, he may have expected an agreement to the decision but Rogan stood his ground that Jared Gordon deserved to win that fight. He “kept it real” according to many Twitter users instead of lowering his head and agreeing to what the guest was saying.

Paddy Pimblett slams his Critics for saying he lost the fight

Paddy The Baddy has not been able to catch a break ever since the decision of his recent fight against Jared Gordon in UFC 282 went his way. Many fight fans have been saying that there was a clear winner – and it was not the rising star.

Paddy has issued a very passionate response to his critics asking them to compare his face and his opponent’s face after the fight. He says that he did more damage to his opponent than his opponent did him, and fights are being scored based on the damage being done and not on the “control time”.

“I don’t care what anyone says. When you look back at the history books, I’ve got a green marker next to my name so everyone else can suck my a**hole,” Pimblett said during the post-fight presser at UFC 282. Pimblett was very passionate and furious at his critics since he “knows” that the fight wasn’t even close to a split decision.
